Nicholas Kerdile Death: How Did Nicholas Kerdiles Pass Away?

September 15, 2024
blog

Nashville, TN – Tragedy struck the hockey community and fans worldwide when former NHL player Nicolas Kerdiles lost his life in a motorcycle accident on Saturday. The 29-year-old ex-player of the Anaheim Ducks met a fatal end following a collision with an SUV in Nashville, police confirmed.

Kerdiles, renowned for his stints with the Ducks and the Manitoba Moose in minor leagues, veered his motorcycle through a stop sign, leading to the catastrophic event. The Metro Nashville Police Department promptly responded, clarifying that there was no impairment involved on either side. The SUV’s driver acted responsibly, bringing the vehicle to an immediate halt. As the investigation unfolds, the police currently do not foresee any charges related to the accident.

A Legacy on Ice: Kerdiles’ Hockey Journey

A native of Irvine, Kerdiles etched a significant mark in hockey history. Drafted by the Ducks in 2012, he not only showcased remarkable skill but also became a symbolic figure. Representing the first player from Orange County to don the Ducks jersey in 2017, he enjoyed a brief yet impactful NHL career. Following his time with the Ducks, he was traded to Winnipeg Jets in 2018. Although his time spent playing hockey was brief, his move into real estate in Nashville indicated a bright second career path.

Sports Community Reacts

The news of his tragic passing sent shockwaves throughout the sports community. The Anaheim Ducks took to social media to express their sorrow, highlighting Kerdiles’ unique status as an Orange County native who graced the team. Their message encapsulated the sentiments of many, emphasizing the weight of this loss for his family, loved ones, and fans.

USA Hockey, a paramount entity in the sport, shared its grief, noting Kerdiles’ invaluable contribution. His commendable achievements with Team USA’s under-18 squad secured two gold medals at the International Ice Hockey Federation’s U18 World Championship. Their social media tribute extended heartfelt condolences to all those mourning this untimely loss.

Collegiate Days: Kerdiles at the University of Wisconsin

Beyond the professional realm, Kerdiles left an indelible mark at the University of Wisconsin, where he played collegiate hockey. The university’s hockey program reminisced about his “great spirit,” portraying a holistic picture of the player, not just limited to his on-field prowess.

Personal Ties: Remembrance from Savannah Chrisley

Outside of his illustrious hockey career, many remember Kerdiles for his once engagement with TV personality Savannah Chrisley of “Chrisley Knows Best.” Their relationship, which ended in 2020, saw both parties sharing numerous cherished moments. Chrisley’s heartfelt social media tribute captured the essence of their bond, emphasizing the depth of her grief and disbelief at the untimely passing of her former fiancé.

As tributes continue to pour in and memories of Kerdiles are shared, the world mourns a talented individual lost too soon. His contributions to the hockey world, his vibrant spirit, and his ability to touch the lives of many will forever be remembered.
