Julius Erving's personal life has had many ups and downs

Julius Erving enjoyed a lot of success as an NBA player. He won three championships (two ABA titles) and recorded many finals appearances. Erving made 16 All-Star appearances (five ABA All-Star selections). He was also a four-time league MVP (three ABA MVPs), and secured multiple All-NBA selections.

Erving also made the NBA's 35th, 50th, and 75th-anniversary teams. Dr. J's excellent career didn't translate into a smooth personal life for the former Philadelphia 76ers legend.

He had multiple kids while being married to his first wife, Turquoise Erving, who had four kids with him during their 31-year-old marriage. Erving's first child outside of marriage was sportswriter Samantha Stevenson. They had a baby girl named Alexandra Stevenson, who became a professional tennis player.

The knowledge of his firstborn child outside marriage was made public after Alexandra made it to the 1999 Wimbledon Finals. Dr. J provided financial support to Alexandra but never developed a relationship with her. They eventually met in 2008 and developed their relationship.
