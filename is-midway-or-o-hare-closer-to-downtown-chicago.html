Is Midway or O’Hare closer to downtown Chicago?

When it comes to choosing between Midway International Airport and O’Hare International Airport in Chicago, one of the first factors to consider is the proximity to downtown. While both airports serve the city, their distances from downtown Chicago differ. So, which airport is closer?

In terms of distance and travel time, Midway Airport is closer to downtown Chicago compared to O’Hare. Located on the city’s southwest side, Midway is approximately 12 miles away from downtown. On a typical day with moderate traffic, it takes around 20-30 minutes to reach the city center from Midway by car or taxi. This relatively shorter distance makes Midway a popular choice for travelers looking for a quicker and more convenient commute to downtown.

On the other hand, O’Hare Airport is situated on the northwest side of the city, about 17 miles away from downtown Chicago. The travel time from O’Hare to downtown can range from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on traffic conditions. While the distance is slightly longer, O’Hare remains a major international airport with a greater number of flights and passenger capacity.

Ultimately, the choice between Midway and O’Hare depends on various factors such as airline preferences, availability of flights, travel itineraries, and personal convenience. Both airports offer a range of transportation options, including public transit, shuttle services, and rental cars, to facilitate the commute to downtown Chicago.
